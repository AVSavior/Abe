Alfredo Pasta

  • Time: 10 Minutes
  • Serves: 2 Cups
Ingredients Quantity
Cup Butter ½ Cup
Heavy Whipped Cream 1½ Cups
Garlic Minced 2 Teaspoon
Italian Seasoning ½ Teaspoon
Salt ½ Teaspoon
Pepper ½ Teaspoon
Freshly Grated Parmesan Cheese 2 Cups

Step 1: Add the butter and cream to a large skillet.

Step 2:Simmer over low heat for 2 minutes.

Step 3:Whisk in the garlic, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper for one minute.

Step 4:Whisk in the parmesan cheese until melted.

Step 5:Serve immediately.

Source: The Salty Marshmallow

Steak

  • Time: 20 Minutes
  • Serves: 1-2
Ingredients Quantity
New York Strip Steaks 2
Vegetable oil ½ Tbsp
Sea Salt 1½ tsp
black pepper, freshly ground 1 tsp
Unsalted butter 2 Tbsp
Cloves garlic, peeled and quartered 2
Sprig fresh rosemary 1

Temp:
Medium Rare (soft, dark pink inside): 145 degrees F
Medium (soft, some pink inside): 160 degrees F
Well Done (very firm, no pink inside): 170 degrees F

Step 1: Pat dry – use paper towels to pat the steaks dry to get a perfect sear and reduce oil splatter.

Step 2:Season generously – just before cooking steaks, sprinkle both sides liberally with salt and pepper.

Step 3:Preheat the pan on medium and brush with oil. Using just 1/2 Tbsp oil reduces splatter.

Step 4:Sear steaks – add steaks and sear each side 3-4 minutes until a brown crust has formed then use tongs to turn steaks on their sides and sear edges (1 min per edge).

Step 5:Add butter and aromatics – melt in butter with quartered garlic cloves and rosemary sprigs. Tilt pan to spoon garlic butter over steaks and cook to your desired doneness (see chart below).

Step 6:Remove steak and rest 10 minutes before slicing against the grain.

Source: Natasha's Kitchen

Burger

  • Time: 23 Minutes
  • Serves: 6 patties
Ingredients Quantity
Ground chuck 2 Pounds
Crushed saltine crackers ½ Cup
Large egg 1
Worcestershire sauce 2 Tablespoons
Milk 2 Tablespoons
Salt 1 Teaspoon
Garlic powder 1 Teaspoons
Onion powder 1 Teaspoons
Black pepper 1 Teaspoons

Step 1: Set out a large mixing bowl. Add in the ground beef, crushed crackers, egg, Worcestershire sauce, milk, salt, garlic powder, onion powder, and pepper. Mix by hand until the meat mixture is smooth, but stop once the mixture looks even. (Overmixing can create a dense heavy texture.)

Step 2:Press the meat down in the bowl, into an even disk. Use a knife to cut and divide the hamburger patty mixture into 6 – 1/3 pound grill or skillet patties, or 12 thin griddle patties.

Step 3:Set out a baking sheet, lined with wax paper or foil, to hold the patties. One at a time, gather the patty mix and press firmly into patties. Shape them just slightly larger than the buns you plan to use, to account for shrinkage during cooking. Set the patties on the baking sheet. Use a spoon to press a dent in the center of each patty so they don't puff up as they cook. If you need to stack the patties separate them with a sheet of wax paper.

Step 4:Preheat the grill or a skillet to medium heat. (Approximately 350-400 degrees F.)

Step 5:
For thick patties: Grill or fry the patties for 3-4 minutes per side.
For thin patties: Cook on the griddle for 2 minutes per side.

Step 6:Stack the hot patties on hamburgers buns, and top with your favorite hamburgers toppings. Serve warm.

Source: A Spicy Perpective
